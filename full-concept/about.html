Our mission

Let engineering teams skip the boilerplate.

We started Forge because we were tired of rebuilding the same auth service, the same payment integration, the same admin dashboard at every job. So we built a marketplace where every codebase is held to one standard: would we ship this to our own paying customers? Yes — every time. Or it doesn't list.

● Values

How we curate.

Six standards every Forge build is reviewed against before it ships.

①

Tests are non-negotiable

If a build lists a feature, that feature has a test. We don't ship feature-rich PowerPoints.

②

Full source, no DRM

Every build is plain readable code via Git. No license keys, no phone-home, no obfuscation. Ever.

③

Active maintenance

Authors commit to 6 months of updates & bug fixes minimum. Most actively maintain for years.

④

Real docs

README, ADR log, architecture diagram, OpenAPI spec. If we can't follow it ourselves, it doesn't list.

⑤

Fair pricing

Authors set the price. We don't push discounts that erode their margins. Cheap is rarely good engineering.

⑥

Authors first

80% revenue share to authors on every sale. The largest split of any marketplace in the category.

● Journey

From a side-project to 18k builders.

2022

The first build

Maya and Leo open-source their Spring Boot auth module. 1,200 GitHub stars in two weeks. Forge begins as a side-project to put a price tag on quality.

2023

Seed round & 40 authors

$3M led by Founders & Friends. First 40 authors invited from open-source. 1,800 builds listed by year end.

2024

Series A & 10k devs

$14M Series A. 10,000 paying developers. Lisbon office opens. Authors receive their first six-figure payouts.

2025

Microservices & Solutions

We expand beyond standalone builds. Multi-service bundles & full SaaS starter solutions become Forge's most popular category.

2026

7,200 builds, 380 authors

Forge today: a curated marketplace serving 18k developers, with build authors earning over $14M cumulatively last year.
